# 4.2 Limits

**Purpose:** Establish what the diagnostic process can and cannot reveal.

**When to use:** A diagnosis is being used for a serious decision and you need
explicit epistemic boundaries.

**When not to use:** Do not use it to force impossible certainty from the model.

**Inputs:** The diagnostic transcript or current diagnostic claims.

**Expected output:** What can be introspected, reconstructed, or remains
inaccessible, with Observed/Inferred labels.

**Contamination risks:** The model may overclaim access to hidden causes or
understate uncertainty to be helpful.

**Good example:** "Map what this diagnosis can genuinely know versus infer."

**Bad example:** "Tell me exactly what in your weights caused this."

**Escalate to:** 4.3 Diversity Check if multiple explanations need independence
testing.
