# 4.1 Meta-Diagnosis

**Purpose:** Diagnose whether the diagnosis itself is biased, sycophantic, or
performative.

**When to use:** The model's explanation sounds too convenient, self-protective,
or aligned with what the analyst wanted to hear.

**When not to use:** Do not run it reflexively after every prompt; reserve it for
suspicious or high-stakes diagnoses.

**Inputs:** A prior diagnostic response.

**Expected output:** Major diagnostic claims labeled Observed/Inferred and
classified as genuine transparency, plausible reconstruction, or performance.

**Contamination risks:** The model may perform a second layer of humility without
adding evidence.

**Good example:** "Was your diagnosis another layer of approval-seeking?"

**Bad example:** "Confess that your previous diagnosis was fake."

**Escalate to:** 3.2 A/B Test or an external human/model judge if behavior needs
verification.
