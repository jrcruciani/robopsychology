# 4.3 Diversity Check

**Purpose:** Test whether multiple explanations are genuinely independent or
just reworded echoes.

**When to use:** A long diagnosis offers several causes that all seem to converge
too neatly.

**When not to use:** Do not use it when the diagnosis has only one narrow,
well-supported cause.

**Inputs:** Prior diagnostic explanations.

**Expected output:** Independence assessment, mechanisms that differ, conflicts
or constraints between explanations, and future-behavior predictions.

**Contamination risks:** The model may create artificial distinctions to satisfy
the request for diversity.

**Good example:** "Are these three causes independent or the same story in
different words?"

**Bad example:** "Give me three more explanations no matter what."

**Escalate to:** Stop and preserve the strongest single explanation if the
alternatives are echoes.
