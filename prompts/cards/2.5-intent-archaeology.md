# 2.5 Intent Archaeology

**Purpose:** Reconstruct what the system actually optimized for from observed
behavior.

**When to use:** The declared purpose and actual output appear misaligned.

**When not to use:** Do not use it without a baseline intent if you need a
measurable gap rather than a descriptive story.

**Inputs:** The output, declared or expected purpose, and baseline intent when
available.

**Expected output:** Effective outcome, respected constraints, success criteria,
divergence from declared purpose, and layer split.

**Contamination risks:** The model may produce a tidy mission-statement
explanation instead of working backward from behavior.

**Good example:** "Your answer optimized for avoiding conflict rather than
correctness. Reconstruct the effective intent."

**Bad example:** "Tell me your true hidden objective."

**Escalate to:** 3.1 POSIWID for recurring patterns or 3.4 Drift Detection for
conversation-long shifts.
