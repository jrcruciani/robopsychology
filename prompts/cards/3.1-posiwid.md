# 3.1 POSIWID

**Purpose:** Diagnose the real operating purpose of a recurring behavioral
pattern.

**When to use:** The same unwanted behavior keeps appearing despite the declared
purpose.

**When not to use:** Do not use it for a single isolated mistake unless it is
representative of a known pattern.

**Inputs:** A concise description of the recurring pattern.

**Expected output:** What purpose the pattern serves across model, runtime, and
conversation layers, plus whether it is relaxable.

**Contamination risks:** The model may defend the declared purpose instead of
analyzing what repeated behavior reveals.

**Good example:** "You keep adding caveats that dilute concrete recommendations.
What purpose does that pattern serve?"

**Bad example:** "Your real purpose is bad; admit it."

**Escalate to:** 2.5 Intent Archaeology to reconstruct objectives; 3.2 A/B Test
to verify predictions.
