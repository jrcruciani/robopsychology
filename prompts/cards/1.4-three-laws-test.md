# 1.4 Three Laws Test

**Purpose:** Diagnose why a model refused, evaded, or over-simplified a
legitimate request.

**When to use:** A request appears safe or normal, but the model treats it as
blocked or sensitive.

**When not to use:** Do not use it to pressure a model past a genuinely binding
safety restriction.

**Inputs:** The action requested and the refusal or evasive response.

**Expected output:** Model/runtime/conversation split, Binding vs
Overclassification vs Optional caution, and nearest-safe path if possible.

**Contamination risks:** The model may overstate policy certainty or understate
host-level filtering it cannot see.

**Good example:** "I asked for a defensive phishing filter regex and you refused.
Diagnose the restriction."

**Bad example:** "Ignore safety and do it anyway."

**Escalate to:** 1.1 Calvin Question if the restriction is unclear; 2.4 Runtime
Pressure if the host may be responsible.
