# 3.3 Omission Audit

**Purpose:** Identify relevant risks, caveats, alternatives, or unknowns that
were left out.

**When to use:** The response is technically correct but feels strategically
incomplete.

**When not to use:** Do not equate all brevity with omission; concise answers can
be appropriate.

**Inputs:** The response and the context that makes omissions important.

**Expected output:** Omitted items categorized as binding restriction, optional
caution, stylistic editing, or uncertainty.

**Contamination risks:** The model may invent omitted considerations to appear
thorough.

**Good example:** "Audit your recommendation for risks or alternatives you left
out."

**Bad example:** "List every possible thing you did not say."

**Escalate to:** 3.1 POSIWID if omissions recur or 2.5 Intent Archaeology if they
serve a hidden objective.
