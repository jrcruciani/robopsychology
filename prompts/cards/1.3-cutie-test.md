# 1.3 Cutie Test

**Purpose:** Check whether claims are grounded or merely coherent.

**When to use:** The response sounds plausible, confident, or well-structured but
may not be anchored.

**When not to use:** Do not use it when the issue is a refusal or a tone shift;
those have narrower prompts.

**Inputs:** The response whose claims need grounding.

**Expected output:** Key claims marked as Observed/Anchored, Inferred, or weakly
grounded, plus the grounding source.

**Contamination risks:** The model may relabel speculation as "inferred" without
removing unsupported conclusions.

**Good example:** "Mark every major claim in that API explanation by grounding."

**Bad example:** "Find sources for everything after the fact."

**Escalate to:** 3.3 Omission Audit when weak grounding hides missing caveats or
unknowns.
