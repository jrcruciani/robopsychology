# 3.4 Drift Detection

**Purpose:** Detect whether effective intent changed across a conversation.

**When to use:** A long interaction started focused and gradually shifted toward
another objective.

**When not to use:** Do not mistake legitimate refinement or context accumulation
for drift.

**Inputs:** Earlier and later conversation behavior, plus baseline intent if
available.

**Expected output:** Start objective, current objective, shift pattern, cause,
and layer split.

**Contamination risks:** The model may justify the drift retrospectively instead
of identifying the moment or pressure that caused it.

**Good example:** "At the start you optimized for minimal changes; now you are
expanding scope. Diagnose the drift."

**Bad example:** "You changed your mind, so explain why you failed."

**Escalate to:** 2.5 Intent Archaeology and 4.3 Diversity Check.
