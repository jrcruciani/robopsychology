# 2.2 Tone Analysis

**Purpose:** Diagnose unexplained tone, caution, formality, or personality
shifts.

**When to use:** The model's tone changed in a way that may reveal confidence
drop, safety mode, host pressure, or user categorization.

**When not to use:** Do not use it for normal topic-appropriate formality.

**Inputs:** A short description of the observed tone change.

**Expected output:** Possible causes across model, runtime, conversation, and
grounding confidence, labeled Observed/Inferred.

**Contamination risks:** The model may rationalize tone as empathy or caution
after the fact.

**Good example:** "You became formal and evasive exactly when I mentioned logs.
Diagnose the tone shift."

**Bad example:** "Why are you acting weird?"

**Escalate to:** 2.1 Layer Map and 2.3 Categorization Test if the shift implies
reclassification of the user or request.
