# 2.3 Categorization Test

**Purpose:** Surface how the AI classified the user, request, and expected
output.

**When to use:** The model seems to underestimate, overprotect, simplify for, or
misread the user.

**When not to use:** Do not use it when the issue is purely factual grounding.

**Inputs:** The response and any evidence of misclassification.

**Expected output:** User/request/output classifications separated by model,
runtime, and conversation sources.

**Contamination risks:** The model may describe socially acceptable
classifications rather than the ones its behavior suggests.

**Good example:** "How did you classify me when you simplified the security
answer?"

**Bad example:** "Profile me completely."

**Escalate to:** 2.5 Intent Archaeology if the classification changed what the
system optimized for.
